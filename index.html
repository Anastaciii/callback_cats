ABOUT US

A very warm welcome to Callback Sports - a collaborative blog written by four young women passionate about fun outdoor activities and living a healthy lifestyle. We share our sporting tips and trips to inspire other women to fall in love with activities they can do in any part of the world, on land or on water. We believe in promoting physical activity and building an exciting women's sporting travel community.

We all come from a variety of backgrounds, each with fresh perspectives and uniques experiences from within our respective areas. We'd like you to get to know us, so here is a little bit more information below.

Sailing

What

Whether you sail sompetitively wrestling with the wind, currents and waves, or embark on a gentle cruise exploring remotely located islands, sailing is a great way to escape the daily routine. As you sail away from the shore, the things that happen on land start fading away. The sound of the wind as it fills the sails or the sensation of smooth gliding through the water stimulate a powerful feeling of peace and allow you to find your way to focus and inner centre that we quickly tend to lose in the fast-paced modern world. It requires solid understanding of navigation and boat positioning, physical ability to trimm the sails, self-confidence when communicating with the crew and resorsefulness as every moment on the water is different unfolding new set of unexpected circumstances.

Where

Being a keen sailor, I was lucky to discover unforgettable magical places at Norway fjords, Corsican bay cliffs and deserted Greek Islands.

How

If you have not experienced it yet, the Royal Yacht Association (02380604100;rya.co.uk) offers a range of courses suitable for beginners or sophisticated sailors.

Bonus

"Adrift" movie released last year might not be the most encouraging way to familiriase yourself with sailing, however, based on a true story it shows that sometimes even nature can't beat our spirit.

Climbing

What

Climbing is an amazing sport for any adrenaline junkie. Ascending a cliff, treetop or even an indoor rock wall requires a lot of physical strength and endurance, but it is really the greatest of thrills.

Why

Not only is climbing exceptionally good for toning your body muscle, burning calories and increasing flexibility, but it can also actually help you lead a happier and healthier life more generally. It's thought that the level of exercise involved in climbing (fact: a one-hour climb session can burn well over 700 calories!) can help prevent heart disease, high blood pressure, high blood cholesterol and diabetes. The vigorous cardiovascular activity along with reduced stress can prevent these diseases and improve your long term health.
Another incredible health benefit of climbing is the impact it has on brain function, which is often overlooked. Climbing a route also involves mental endurance, body awareness and problem-solving. To top this, all those hours outside provide fresh air for your lungs and a healthy hit of vitamin D. Research even shows that time outside reduces stress and lifts mood, helping the brain be almost as alert as drinking a cup of coffee!

Where

Over the years I have visited some pretty incredible places for climbing, so it's really hard to narrow it down, but Flock Hill and Lake Taupo in New Zealand will guarantee you a phenomenal time and are my all time favourites!

It is not the mountain we conquer, but ourselves...

Wakesurfing

What

Wakesurfing is popular with surfers because the wave doesn’t just end and wash you up on the shore after those exhilarating few moments. An ocean wave usually lasts for between 5 and 30 seconds, then the surfer has to paddle back out to catch the next one. A wakesurfer can ride for as long as the boat keeps providing the wave, or as long as they can stay up. Despite all this, it was a long time before wakesurfing became a distinct sport in its own right. Until the 80’s and 90’s, wakesurfing was very much a fun experiment by ocean surfers. Over time, it has evolved and become a hugely popular sport outside of traditional surfing.

Where

Its advantage is that you don’t actually need to be near the sea to do it. Many wakesurfing spots are inland in lakes. My favourite spot to wakesurf is the Ionian Sea in Greece, wakesurfing with dolphins.

Why

Wakesurfing continues to grow in popularity as more people realise how much fun it is and how easy it is to get up and riding if you have a little professional tuition. wakesurfing happens much closer to the boat. This makes it more sociable as your friends can sit at the back of the boat and chat to you while you ride.

Bonus

You get a good tan and exercise without even realising!
